why are we working with a map?
draft answer, August 14, 2022


a map shows where you live or maybe where you would like to live.

a map is a representation of how a politico-socio-economic system interacts with the physical environment, but for our demographic, for which there is little politico-socio-economic space and less and less physical space, a map is a destabilizing reminder of erasure. maybe this is why we are working with a map.

when i open a map of Toronto, i see a story but there is no "home".

there must be a way to change the existing story so that there is "home".

how?

we have chosen to use this map for a very specific goal and to work with adding words to it, written (letters) and/or said out loud (sound). we trust that there is power in our verbalizations, voices and ideas.

can we work with a map however without participating in the colonization that they have traditionally enabled? this question simply highlights our responsibility to participate in de-colonization.
